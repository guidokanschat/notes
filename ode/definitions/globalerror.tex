\begin{Definition}{global error}
  Let $u$ be a solution of the differential equation $u' = f(t,u)$
  on the interval $I_n = [t_{n-1}, t_{n}]$. Then, the \define{global
    error} of a discrete method $F$ is the difference between the
  solution $u_n$ of the differential equation at $t_n$ and the result
  of the one-step method at $t_n$, i.e.
  \begin{gather} \label{eq:explicit:5a} % Ja, ist unbenutzt (LS)
    u(t_n) - y(t_n).
  \end{gather}
\end{Definition}
